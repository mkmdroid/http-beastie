About Me

I am Mohamed Mohamed, a Computer Engineering student at the American University in Cairo (AUC), where I study on a full scholarship. I’m a graduate of STEM High School for Boys – 6th of October, an Egyptian boarding school that nurtures the country’s brightest talents.

At STEM, I developed strong teamwork skills through semester‑long capstone engineering projects and club activities. I joined the physics club and later mentored younger students who shared my passion. This experience deepened my understanding of physics and helped me earn 3rd place in the Egyptian Physics Olympiad (EPhO) 2025.

Through hard work and God’s grace, I also placed 3rd nationally in the Math section of the high school STEM track, which led to my scholarship at AUC. That achievement, combined with advice from experts in academia and industry, convinced me to shift my focus from physics to computer science.

Now I strive to work hard and be a positive role model for my younger friends and for my two brothers, who are currently in eleventh grade at the same high school.
